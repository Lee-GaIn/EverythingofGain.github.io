Chatbot is a computer program which allows people to communicate with computers as a human by using various forms like voice and message to automate the operation process. The chatbot mainly has two types which are take-driven chatbot and data-driven and predictive chatbot. The main difference between take-driven chatbot and data-driven chatbot is variability. Most take-driven chatbots usually conduct singular and basic tasks like FAQs. This chatbot tends to generate static responses because it returns the standard responses based on the rules made by developers. It means that the chatbot will probably perform well the more requests are simple, specific and typically similar to the developer's expectation to the users. The example of take-driven chatbots are easily found in e-commerce websites, on facebook messenger and on a live chat. The chatbot type is the most common type although this chatbot type looks like it has a number of limitations in variability and flexibility of command. On the other hand, data-driven and predictive chatbot can handle unprogrammed situations by using databases from user past behavior and their profile. They can not only understand natural language that people normally use unlike computer language but also use predicted intelligence to offer personalized optimization to each user. They are frequently used to provide recommendations to users and even predict their needs based on preferences. The examples of these models are Apple’s Siri, Amazon’s Alexa and Google home.
